Frequently Asked Questions

Find answers to common questions about plant diseases, treatments, and our AI classification system

Our AI classification system has been trained on over 54,000 images across 38 different plant disease classes. It achieves an average accuracy of 98.6% on the PlantVillage dataset. However, real-world conditions may vary, which is why we also provide a confidence score with each prediction and recommend expert review for cases with low confidence or severe diseases.
For the best results, upload clear, well-lit images that show the affected plant parts in detail. Include multiple images if possible, showing both healthy and diseased portions for comparison. Close-ups of symptoms like leaf spots, wilting, or discoloration are particularly helpful. If you know the plant species, including this information can improve classification accuracy.
Different plant diseases often have distinctive visual symptoms. Fungal diseases typically present as spots, blotches, or powdery/fuzzy growth. Bacterial diseases often create water-soaked lesions with yellow halos. Viral diseases commonly cause mottling, mosaic patterns, or leaf deformation. Our Disease Library section provides detailed guides with images to help you distinguish between various plant diseases based on their visual characteristics.
Organic treatments use naturally-derived substances like neem oil, copper, sulfur, or biological controls like beneficial microorganisms. They typically have less environmental impact but may require more frequent application. Chemical treatments use synthesized compounds developed specifically for disease control. They often provide more immediate and long-lasting control but may have greater environmental impacts and residue concerns. Our treatment recommendations provide both options with guidance on when each is most appropriate.
To prevent the spread of plant diseases: (1) Remove and dispose of infected plant material promptly; (2) Disinfect gardening tools between plants; (3) Avoid working with plants when they're wet; (4) Maintain proper spacing between plants for good air circulation; (5) Use drip irrigation instead of overhead watering; (6) Practice crop rotation; (7) Choose disease-resistant varieties when available; and (8) Monitor plants regularly for early signs of disease.
